Ten of Diamonds

Cherry blossom raindrop cake

Description

Raindrop cake is a Japanese dessert known as mizu shingen mochi. It is a jelly-like dessert made only with mineral water and agar powder, and resembles a giant raindrop. Because it is made of mostly water and a little agar powder, it has almost no calories. It is traditionally served with kinako (roasted soy bean flour) and kuromitsu, a Japanese black sugar syrup that literally translates as “black honey.” The raindrop cake dissolves in your mouth, which is a unique and fun sensation. When eaten with the syrup and flour, this dessert is refreshing, light and sweet.
